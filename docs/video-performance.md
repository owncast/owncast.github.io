---
title: Video quality and performance
description: How broadcasting software, video settings, object storage, latency, and metrics combine to shape your stream.
---

Stream quality is a chain that runs from the software you broadcast with to the way viewers pull video. Each page here covers one link in that chain.

Your [broadcasting software](/docs/broadcasting) sets the resolution, framerate, and bitrate that Owncast receives. The [video quality settings](/docs/video) then control the output qualities Owncast produces and the hardware cost of producing them, since more qualities and higher bitrates mean more CPU.

[Object storage](/docs/storage) moves the bandwidth of serving video off your own server onto an S3 compatible provider, which starts to matter as your audience grows. [Reducing latency](/docs/latency) is the tradeoff between how close to live your stream runs and how smoothly it plays. [Stream performance](/docs/metrics) shows from the admin whether the result is holding up.
